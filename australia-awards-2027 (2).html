Who Can Apply

Africa-Specific Eligibility

African applicants have additional country-specific criteria beyond the general Australia Awards requirements. All conditions must be met.

⚠️

Critical: Project Management is NOT a priority field for Africa. You must apply for a master's program that aligns with one of the 5 approved priority fields. Frame your existing project management degree as a professional strength to be applied within that field.

✅You Must Meet All of These
✓Be at least 25 years old on 1 February 2027
✓Have a minimum of 5 years post-graduate work experience in a field related to your area of study
✓Be currently employed at the time of applying for the scholarship
✓Be a citizen of an eligible African country and residing and applying from that country
✓Already hold an undergraduate degree (equivalent to an Australian undergraduate degree)
✓Meet the minimum English language proficiency (IELTS 6.5 / TOEFL 84 / PTE 58)

For your 2nd Class Honours BA: This meets the undergraduate degree requirement. It maps to approximately a 65–75% credit average in the Australian grading system — strong enough for most postgraduate programs.

❌You Must NOT Have
✗Already hold or be studying towards a master's degree or equivalent
✗Previously received a long-term Australia Award
✗Be an Australian citizen or hold permanent residency in Australia
✗Be married to, engaged to, or in a de facto partnership with an Australian or New Zealand citizen or permanent resident
✗Be applying for a visa to live permanently in Australia

⚠️ The partner restriction applies throughout the entire application, selection, and mobilisation phase — not just at the time of applying. Concealing this information results in immediate disqualification.

💡

Post-Award Obligation: If awarded the scholarship, you must leave Australia for a minimum of two years after completing your studies before you can return on a permanent visa. This is a binding contractual obligation you sign before departure.

Step-by-Step Process

How to Apply — 8 Steps

Follow this exact sequence to maximise your chances of success.

1
Now — January 2026

Confirm Your Eligibility

Verify you are: (a) at least 25 on 1 Feb 2027, (b) have 5+ years post-graduate work experience in your target field, (c) currently employed, (d) citizen and resident of an eligible African country, and (e) hold only an undergraduate degree. If you have a master's already, you are not eligible.

2
Now — January 2026

Take Your English Language Test

Book and sit your IELTS, TOEFL iBT, or PTE Academic test. Scores must be valid on 1 January 2026 (not 2027). This means your test must be taken no more than 2 years before that date. Minimum: IELTS 6.5 (no band below 6.0). This step is exempt only if your first language is English and your education was conducted entirely in English.

3
December 2025 — January 2026

Choose Your Field & University

Select one of the 5 Africa priority fields (see fields section above). Research 2–3 universities from the table that offer master's programs in that field. Ensure your chosen program aligns with African development priorities. Universities like ANU, University of Melbourne, University of Queensland, and Monash offer programs across multiple fields.

4
1 February 2026 (09:00 AEDT)

Application Opens — Start Your OASIS Profile

Register at oasispartners.dfat.gov.au. Use your full legal name exactly as shown on your passport — including all middle names. No nicknames or abbreviations. Prepare your Curriculum Vitae using the Australia Awards template. All applications must be submitted through OASIS.

5
February — April 2026

Compile & Upload Your Documents

Compile documents into single PDFs by category. Name files clearly (e.g. "Certificates.pdf", "Referee Reports.pdf"). All scans must be: in colour, legible, correct page order, correct orientation. Required documents: Passport, Undergraduate Degree Certificate, Academic Transcript, Curriculum Vitae, Employer Referee Report, Academic Referee Report, and English test certificate. All must be certified.

6
30 April 2026 (14:00 AEST)

Submit Before the Deadline — No Exceptions

Applications and supporting documents received after 14:00 AEST on 30 April 2026 will NOT be considered under any circumstances. Do not wait until the final day. Submit at least 48–72 hours before the deadline to allow for technical issues. Late submissions will be rejected even if only a few minutes late.

7
May — October 2026

Shortlisting & Interview

Applications are first checked for eligibility. Shortlisted candidates only are contacted — if you do not hear back, you were not shortlisted. The interview assesses: (1) academic competence, (2) potential contribution to development outcomes in Africa, and (3) professional and personal leadership. Prepare with concrete examples from your work experience.

8
Late 2026

Results, Pre-Departure & Travel to Australia

Successful candidates notified in late 2026. You must attend: course counselling, pre-departure briefing, and the university's Introductory Academic Program (IAP) on arrival in Australia. Sign the scholarship contract committing to return home for at least two years after completion. Study commences in Australia in 2027.
